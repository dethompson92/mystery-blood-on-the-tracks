⚖️ Content & Sensitivity Notes

Important Context

Historical Accuracy: These documents reflect 1957 attitudes, laws, and social norms regarding LGBTQ+ individuals. In 1957, same-sex relationships were criminalized, pathologized as mental illness, and subjected to extreme social persecution ("Lavender Scare").

Sympathetic Portrayal: While the murderer (Charlotte Devereaux) committed an unforgivable crime, the story acknowledges the societal oppression that created desperation and fear. Both victim and killer were casualties of a cruelly homophobic era.

Educational Value: This mystery teaches about: (1) Evolution of forensic science (DNA analysis), (2) Historical LGBTQ+ persecution, (3) How hidden relationships complicate investigations, (4) Locked-room mystery solving, (5) Cold case investigation techniques.

Appropriate Audiences: Mature content suitable for ages 16+ with proper historical context. Trigger warnings: murder/violence, LGBTQ+ persecution, discussion of suicide (brief historical context only), era-appropriate discriminatory attitudes.
