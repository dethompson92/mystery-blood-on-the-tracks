WITNESS STATEMENT (Question and Answer Format)

DETECTIVE HOLLOWAY: Miss Devereaux, please state your full name, age, and occupation for the record.
DEVEREAUX: Charlotte Marie Devereaux. I go by "Lottie" professionally. I'm twenty-nine years old. I'm an actress—I've appeared in several films and television programs in Hollywood over the past seven years.
HOLLOWAY: What was your reason for traveling on the Silver Comet train from Chicago to New Orleans?
DEVEREAUX: I was going home for Thanksgiving. I'm originally from Louisiana—Baton Rouge. My family still lives there. I was planning to spend the holiday with them in New Orleans.
[FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Journal entries dated November 10-12, 1957, indicate subject's true purpose was to pursue Vivienne Marchand and attempt to reconcile their romantic relationship. Subject deliberately concealed this motive.]
HOLLOWAY: Were you acquainted with the deceased, Miss Vivienne Marchand, prior to boarding the train?
DEVEREAUX: Only very slightly, Detective. We'd met a few times in Los Angeles at jazz clubs. She was a singer, I believe. We'd exchanged pleasantries, but we weren't friends or anything like that. Just acquaintances.
[FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Recovered love letters (47 letters, dated 1954-1957) and journal entries document an intimate three-year romantic relationship between subject and victim. Photographs show the two women together in private settings, embracing. Subject deliberately misrepresented relationship as "casual acquaintances."]
HOLLOWAY: Did you interact with Miss Marchand during the train journey?
DEVEREAUX: Not really. I saw her in the lounge car briefly on the first evening. We nodded hello, but we didn't speak. I think she was with a gentleman—her fiancé, perhaps? I kept to myself mostly. I wasn't feeling well.
HOLLOWAY: You weren't feeling well? Can you elaborate?
DEVEREAUX: I had a headache, and I was exhausted from travel. I spent most of the evening in my compartment. I retired early, around nine-thirty or ten o'clock.
[PARTIAL FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Porter Ernest Washington reported serving subject two glasses of bourbon in lounge car around 9:00 PM. Bartender Marcus Webb confirmed subject appeared intoxicated. Subject was not in compartment as early as claimed.]
HOLLOWAY: Your compartment was Compartment 8 in the Magnolia sleeper car, correct? That's directly adjacent to Miss Marchand's compartment, Compartment 7.
DEVEREAUX: Yes, that's correct. I didn't realize we were neighbors until I saw the compartment assignments. Pure coincidence.
HOLLOWAY: Did you hear anything unusual from Miss Marchand's compartment during the night? Any sounds, voices, or disturbances?
DEVEREAUX: No, nothing. I fell asleep around ten-thirty and slept straight through until morning. The train rocks you to sleep, you know. I didn't hear a thing.
[FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Porter Ernest Washington observed light under subject's compartment door at 3:10 AM. Witness Margaret Sinclair heard two women's voices (identified as victim and another female) arguing in victim's compartment at 2:40-2:50 AM, followed by sudden silence and footsteps. Journal entry dated November 14, 1957, 4:00 AM, contains full confession to entering victim's compartment at 2:30 AM and strangling victim with silk scarf at approximately 2:46 AM.]
HOLLOWAY: At what time did you wake up in the morning?
DEVEREAUX: The porter knocked on my door around seven o'clock. That's when I woke up. A few minutes later, I heard commotion in the corridor. People talking urgently. I opened my door and saw the porter outside Miss Marchand's compartment. He looked very upset. He told us she'd... she'd passed away. I was shocked.
HOLLOWAY: How did you react when you learned of Miss Marchand's death?
DEVEREAUX: I was stunned, Detective. Even though we weren't close, it's still shocking to learn someone you know has died, especially in such proximity. I felt terrible. She was so young and talented.
[DECEPTIVE PERFORMANCE - 2024 EVIDENCE: Witness Margaret Sinclair reported seeing subject at breakfast appearing "pale, shaken, like she'd seen a ghost... her hands were trembling." Porter Ernest Washington noted subject seemed "visibly disturbed." Subject was displaying genuine guilt and fear of discovery, not shock at learning of death she had caused.]
HOLLOWAY: Miss Devereaux, did you leave your compartment at any time during the night between when you went to bed and when the porter woke you in the morning?
DEVEREAUX: No, sir. I stayed in my compartment all night. I was asleep.
[FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Subject exited victim's compartment at approximately 2:55 AM carrying murder weapon (silk scarf). Witness Margaret Sinclair heard compartment door close softly and footsteps in corridor at that time. Subject returned to own compartment and hid scarf in luggage.]
HOLLOWAY: The victim was last seen wearing a distinctive burgundy and gold silk scarf. That scarf is now missing from her belongings. Do you know anything about it?
DEVEREAUX: A scarf? No, I don't know anything about that. I'm sorry, Detective, I wish I could be more helpful, but I really don't have any information about what happened to Miss Marchand.
[FALSE STATEMENT - 2024 EVIDENCE: Subject removed silk scarf from crime scene at approximately 2:55 AM on November 14, 1957. Scarf was murder weapon used to strangle victim. Subject kept scarf hidden in locked trunk for 67 years. DNA analysis (2024) confirmed victim's blood and subject's touch DNA on scarf. Subject gave victim the scarf as gift in December 1955 (journal entry confirms). Scarf recovered November 3, 2024, from subject's residence.]
HOLLOWAY: Did you observe any tensions or conflicts between passengers during the journey? Anything that might be relevant?
DEVEREAUX: Well, I did overhear Miss Marchand and that gentleman—Mr. DeLuca, I think his name was—having a somewhat heated discussion in the lounge car on Wednesday evening. Their voices were raised. It seemed like a lovers' quarrel or something. I didn't pay much attention to it, but other people noticed too.
[STRATEGIC MISDIRECTION - 2024 EVIDENCE: Subject deliberately emphasized argument between victim and Vincent DeLuca to deflect suspicion toward him. This was calculated misdirection. Subject successfully shifted investigators' focus to DeLuca as primary suspect, allowing subject to avoid detection.]
HOLLOWAY: Is there anything else you'd like to add to this statement? Anything you observed or remember that might help our investigation?
DEVEREAUX: No, Detective. I wish I could be of more help. I hope you find whoever did this. Miss Marchand didn't deserve to die like that. It's a tragedy.
HOLLOWAY: Thank you for your cooperation, Miss Devereaux. We may contact you for additional questions as the investigation proceeds. Please provide your contact information in Los Angeles.
DEVEREAUX: Of course. I'll provide my address and telephone number. I'm returning to Los Angeles as soon as you release me to travel. I hope you catch the person responsible.
[INVESTIGATIVE OUTCOME - 1957: Subject was released and never charged. Investigators focused investigation on Vincent DeLuca and other male suspects. Subject's deception was successful.
