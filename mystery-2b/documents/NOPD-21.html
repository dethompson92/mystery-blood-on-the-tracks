Orleans Parish District Attorney's Office

Case Summary - Historical Homicide

Case Summary: NOPD-COLD-2024-0847

Summary: On November 18, 2024, Charlotte Devereaux confessed to the 1957 murder of Vivienne Marchand aboard the Silver Comet train. Confession was corroborated by physical evidence (recovered murder weapon with victim's DNA) and documentary evidence (journal, love letters, train ticket).

Legal Determination: No criminal prosecution possible—suspect deceased November 24, 2024. However, case is officially solved and closed. Victim's family has been notified and provided with complete investigation report.

Historical Context Statement: This case occurred during an era when same-sex relationships were criminalized and subjected to extreme social stigma. While this context does not excuse murder, it provides essential understanding of the circumstances that led to this tragedy. The District Attorney's office recognizes the profound injustices faced by LGBTQ+ individuals in mid-20th century America.

Signed: Jason R. Williams, District Attorney, Orleans Parish
Date: November 30, 2024
