I. Executive Summary

Latent fingerprint analysis was performed on Exhibit EX-007-CORD, a 2.4-meter black extension cord recovered from the rooftop infinity pool drain at Gran Horizonte Resort & Spa in connection with Case FGE-QR-2025-07-0847 (death of Solomon Vance, DOD: July 19, 2025). The cord was found looped around the base of the Conair Pro Series 1875W hair dryer (Exhibit EX-006-HDD) used as the murder instrument.

This laboratory identified three recoverable latent print deposits on the cord's outer rubber sheathing. Full analysis using cyanoacrylate fuming, fingerprint powder enhancement, and digital comparison against the AFIS-Mexico national registry and voluntary sample database yielded one positive identification of strong evidentiary value.

⚠ Critical Finding

A partial left-hand thumbprint deposit (Print C — designated FP-C-CORD) recovered from the proximal male plug-end of the extension cord produced a 14-point minutiae match to the voluntary elimination sample provided by subject VICTOR DELGADO (DOB: August 3, 1982). This match exceeds the minimum 12-point threshold required for positive identification under Mexican federal forensic standards (NOM-032-SSA3-2010).

III. Methods & Procedures

All exhibits were received in sealed evidence packaging on July 19, 2025 at 08:45 hrs from Investigator Lic. Ramírez-Ortiz (FGE-QR). The following sequential processing was applied to Exhibit EX-007-CORD:

  • Step 1 — Exhibits air-dried at ambient temperature (22°C) in a sterile enclosure for 4 hours before processing to prevent hydrolysis damage to latent deposits.
  • Step 2 — Cyanoacrylate (Super Glue) fuming in sealed chamber, 65% humidity, 25°C, for 90 minutes. Developed three visible latent ridge deposits (A, B, C).
  • Step 3 — Black fingerprint powder enhancement applied to Deposits A and C (lighter surface areas). Rhodamine 6G fluorescent dye applied to Deposit B (darker grip area).
  • Step 4 — High-resolution digital photography (6,000 DPI) under raking white light and UV illumination (395nm). Images processed using FOXTROT AFIS enhancement software v4.2.
  • Step 5 — Minutiae extraction and mapping: ridge endings, bifurcations, short ridges, enclosures, and dots were catalogued per INTERPOL standard classification.
  • Step 6 — Automated AFIS-Mexico query conducted against 14.7 million registered prints. Negative result for Deposits A and B. Deposit C returned one candidate match (confidence threshold > 85%).
  • Step 7 — Manual verification of AFIS candidate for Deposit C performed by two independent examiners (Lic. Valdés-Cruz and Dra. Herrera-Salas). Consensus: 14-point match confirmed.

V. Contextual Analysis & Significance

The position of FP-C-CORD on the male plug end of the extension cord is forensically significant. This grip position is consistent with a deliberate insertion action — specifically, pressing the plug into a wall or extension socket — rather than incidental contact during routine handling. The cord was not an amenity accessible from the pool area; it originated from Equipment Room B1, a keycard-restricted service corridor.

Victor Delgado's voluntary elimination sample was provided at 06:15 hrs on July 19, 2025 along with those of all other resort guests and production staff. His sample was submitted on standard FGE-QR fingerprint card FPC-2025-0847-006. He stated at the time of sampling that he had "never been in Equipment Room B1" and had "no reason to handle maintenance equipment." This statement is irreconcilable with the forensic finding.

✓ Forensic Conclusion

Victor Delgado physically handled Extension Cord Exhibit EX-007-CORD at the male plug end. His own statement that he had never accessed Equipment Room B1 or handled maintenance equipment is demonstrably false. This physical evidence, combined with the keycard access log (EX-004-KCARD), places Victor Delgado in possession of the murder instrument.
