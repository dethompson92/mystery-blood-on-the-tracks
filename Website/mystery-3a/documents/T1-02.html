Summary
Incident Summary

At approximately 01:15 AM on July 20, 2025, the body of a 33-year-old male American contestant was discovered face-down in the main swimming pool of the Gran Horizonte Resort & Spa, Cancún, Mexico. The victim was a participant in the American reality television program Forever Match, produced by Solara Entertainment Group, currently filming Season 4 at the resort. Responding officers noted the presence of a hair dryer connected to an extension cord at the pool's edge. The pool area's GFCI circuit breaker had been manually disabled. The death is being investigated as a suspected homicide.

Where It Happened

Gran Horizonte Resort & Spa

Blvd. Kukulcán Km 14.5, Zona Hotelera, Cancún, Quintana Roo, México 77500.
5-star all-inclusive resort currently under partial exclusive production contract with Solara Entertainment Group for the duration of Forever Match Season 4 filming (July 1–28, 2025).

The incident occurred at the Main Pool (Zone B) — a 25-meter free-form pool on the resort's south terrace, accessible from the pool bar and the residential production wing. The pool area is covered by two surveillance cameras: C-7 (pool deck, east side) and C-8 (pool terrace entry). Both cameras were offline at the time of death.

Established Facts

What the Evidence Confirms

21:00 Poolside gathering. Cast witnesses a public altercation between two contestants. One contestant makes a verbal threat directed at the victim, overheard by multiple witnesses.
22:00 A group of contestants departs for the resort dining room for a scheduled late dinner. They remain there until after midnight.
22:15 Surveillance cameras covering the main pool (C-7) and pool terrace (C-8) go offline. No scheduled maintenance was logged for this period.
23:02 Victim posts a selfie to Instagram from the pool bar area. Caption: "Pool party for one. 🌊 #ForeverMatch." This is his last confirmed living communication.
~00:05 Based on preliminary forensic assessment, death likely occurred in this window. A hair dryer attached to an extension cord was found at the pool's edge, still connected to a service outlet. The GFCI circuit for this outlet had been manually bypassed.
01:00 Surveillance cameras C-7 and C-8 come back online. No footage of the pool area exists for the 2 hour 45 minute outage window.
01:15 Night-shift pool attendant discovers the victim's body in the pool. Security is alerted. Emergency services arrive at 01:29.
For Investigators

Questions to Pursue

Investigator Focus Areas
  • Q1The camera outage lasted nearly 3 hours. Was this a technical failure or a deliberate act — and who had the ability to cause it?
  • Q2The hair dryer found at the scene is not a guest room unit. Where did it come from, and who had access to it?
  • Q3The GFCI circuit was manually disabled. This requires physical access to an electrical panel. Where is that panel located, and who could reach it that night?
  • Q4A public threat was made against the victim earlier that evening. Is the threatening party actually responsible — or are they a distraction?
  • Q5Why was the victim alone at the pool after midnight? Did he arrange to meet someone there?
