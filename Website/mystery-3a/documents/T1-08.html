Overview

Dominic Wallace, 31, is a personal trainer and former Division II collegiate football player from Atlanta, Georgia. He operates his own personal training business in Charlotte, NC. He is the algorithmic match of contestant Zara Mirza. By all accounts, Dominic applied to Forever Match sincerely — looking for a genuine relationship, not fame or exposure.

On camera at 9:47 PM on July 18, 2025 — less than three hours before Solomon Vance's estimated time of death — Dominic confronted Solomon in the pool bar area and made the following documented statement. This is the most explicit verbal threat in the case record.

"You're a snake. You play games with real people's feelings. You're dead to me, you know that? Dead to me."
— Dominic Wallace, directed at Solomon Vance · 9:47 PM · July 18, 2025 · Captured on Production Cameras P-04 and P-07

Dominic's background is athletic and professionally focused. Former collegiate football at the Division II level, now running a successful fitness business. Multiple show staff described him as "the most genuine person on the cast" — someone who treated the show's premise seriously and was visibly invested in building an authentic relationship with his algorithmically matched partner, Zara Mirza.

⚡ Relationship to Victim & Documented Conflict

Dominic's conflict with Solomon Vance stems directly from Solomon's secret interactions with Zara Mirza — Dominic's assigned algorithm match. Dominic discovered that Solomon had been privately meeting with Zara outside of official filming schedules, in apparent violation of the show's contestant interaction protocols.

After learning of this, Dominic confronted Solomon on the evening of July 18 in front of witnesses and active production cameras. The confrontation was emotionally charged but not physically violent. Investigators note that Dominic made no physical contact with Solomon during the confrontation and left the area after approximately 90 seconds.

🔍 Documented Motive (as known to investigators) Romantic betrayal. Dominic believed Solomon was deliberately undermining his genuine connection with Zara Mirza for personal entertainment or leverage. His verbal threat, while recorded, must be weighed against his physical and emotional profile, his alibi claims, and the broader evidence pattern.
