I. Scene Conditions at Time of Examination

The decedent was discovered in the north quadrant of the rooftop infinity pool (Floor 15, North Tower), submerged to approximately waist depth in a face-up supine position. Water temperature was 29°C (84°F). Pool lights were active at time of discovery. An electrical appliance (subsequently identified as a Conair 1875W hair dryer, Model CDSH1875, serial SN-4472-B) was found at the pool floor adjacent to the decedent's right leg. A 25-foot extension cord was found coiled near the pool access gate, partially wet.

The main circuit breaker for the pool area electrical outlets was confirmed in the ON position by resort engineering staff. The pool area exterior outlet (mounted on the northeast equipment panel, 3m from the pool edge) showed burn marks consistent with high-amperage discharge.

II. External Examination Findings
Primary Findings — Electrical Injury Consistent
1.Entry burn marks: Two distinct low-voltage entry wound sites identified on the right foot (plantar surface, arch) and left hand (palmar surface, ring and little finger). Sites are characterized by dry, leathery, gray-brown tissue with central charring. Approximate diameter: 1.5cm and 1.8cm respectively. Pattern consistent with electrical current entry through submerged extremities.
2.Arborescent markings: Feathering or Lichtenberg figures visible on right forearm and lower right leg — branching skin markings consistent with electrical injury in living tissue, indicating the victim was alive at time of current passage.
3.Cyanosis: Perioral cyanosis (bluish discoloration around lips and oral mucosa) and peripheral cyanosis at fingertips, consistent with cardiopulmonary arrest.
4.No drowning indicators (preliminary): Absence of froth at mouth and nasal passages. No foam cone at nostrils. Pulmonary status to be confirmed at full autopsy, but external presentation does not suggest primary drowning as cause of death.
5.No traumatic injury: No blunt force trauma, incised wounds, gunshot wounds, or strangulation marks identified on external examination. Neck: no petechiae consistent with asphyxia. No defensive wounds on hands.
6.Lividity (livor mortis): Fixed dorsal lividity consistent with supine position — indicates decedent did not move significantly after death. Position at discovery likely reflects position of death.
III. Preliminary Cause & Manner of Death
Preliminary Opinion — Dr. Arturo Villanueva-Restrepo, SEMEFO Q. Roo

Based on external examination findings, scene conditions, and the presence of an active electrical appliance in the pool water, the preliminary cause of death is: Ventricular fibrillation secondary to low-voltage electrocution (accidental-appearing).

Manner of death: The physical evidence is inconsistent with accidental death. A powered hair dryer does not fall into a rooftop pool from 3 meters distant without assistance. The placement of the dryer, the presence of the extension cord coiled near the gate, and the absence of any legitimate reason for the appliance to be in the pool area are collectively consistent with intentional introduction of the device into the water while the victim was present. Preliminary manner of death: HOMICIDE.

Full determination pending complete autopsy, toxicology screening (72 hours), and analysis of electrical appliance.

V. Investigative Notes from Forensic Examiner

The pattern of injury in this case — ventricular fibrillation from aquatic electrocution, no trauma, calm posture at time of death — is consistent with a victim who was unaware of the lethal mechanism until it was activated. The absence of defensive wounds and the calm posture support the hypothesis that the victim was relaxed, seated, with lower extremities in the water at the time the current was introduced.

Hair dryers of this wattage (1875W) generate sufficient current density at pool resistance levels to induce cardiac arrest within 15–60 seconds. Death would have been rapid. The victim likely experienced initial muscle tetany (involuntary contraction) before loss of consciousness and cardiac arrest. He would not have been able to call for help or exit the pool once the current was established.

NOTE FOR INVESTIGATORS: Evidence item E-001 (hair dryer cord) has been prioritized for fingerprint analysis. Preliminary visual inspection in the field identified what may be a partial fingerprint on the rubber-grip section of the cord, approximately 15cm from the plug end. This has been preserved and sent for latent print analysis. Results expected within 24–48 hours.
