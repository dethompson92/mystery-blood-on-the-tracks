Overview

Vivian Fong, 27, is the Associate Producer of Forever Match, employed by Stellar Content Studios, Los Angeles. A UCLA Film School graduate with two years of prior production experience, this is her first major show credit. She is responsible for coordinating daily production logistics, managing the contestant schedule, and overseeing technical operations including the camera system.

Vivian is the only production staff member among the six persons of interest. Her significance to the investigation is twofold: she had professional access to the resort's technical infrastructure, and she was reportedly in Camera Control Room B during the camera malfunction that blacked out 38 cameras in the North Tower area — the same window in which Solomon Vance was killed.

⚠ Key Investigative Fact The North Tower camera system experienced a malfunction at 10:02 PM that eliminated surveillance coverage of the pool area, stairwells, and equipment room for approximately 3 hours. Vivian Fong's role gives her both the technical knowledge and the system access to influence the camera infrastructure. Investigators are examining whether the malfunction was accidental or deliberate.

⚡ Relationship to Victim & Documented Conflict

Solomon Vance interacted with Vivian professionally and, according to Vivian's own account, privately. She states that in Week 2, Solomon approached her and claimed to have analyzed data from the production system and had "questions about how the algorithm actually works." She says she rebuffed his inquiry and reported the interaction to her superior.

Production records show that a contestant-facing data display terminal was accessed with Solomon's credentials during Week 2 at times outside of normal contestant usage patterns. The nature and extent of what Solomon may have accessed is under active investigation.

"He was fishing for something. I told him the algorithm data isn't for contestants to analyze. He smiled and said, 'I already did.' I didn't know what that meant." — Vivian Fong, initial statement

🗣 Statement to Investigators (Initial)
"I was in the control room all night. The system crashed — 38 cameras went down and I was trying to find the source. I had nothing to do with what happened to Solomon. I barely knew him outside of production interactions."

Vivian's keycard log supports her claim that she did not physically leave Camera Control Room B between 10:08 PM and 2:33 AM. However, investigators note that the control room contains terminals with remote access to resort systems. Her physical presence in the room does not necessarily account for all of her technical activity during that window.

🔎 Investigator's Notes — Points to Examine

· Vivian's keycard log appears to support her alibi. But the camera malfunction — within her domain of responsibility — raises questions: was it accidental or triggered?

· What specific systems can be accessed from Camera Control Room B terminals? Does this include the resort's electrical systems?

· Solomon claimed to have accessed production data. Does Vivian know what he found? Did that information create a professional threat to her?

· Vivian reported Solomon's "data fishing" to a superior. Who is that superior, and what action, if any, was taken?

· Cross-reference the timeline: the malfunction began at 10:02 PM. Vivian entered the control room at 10:08 PM — 6 minutes after the malfunction started. Why wasn't she alerted immediately?
