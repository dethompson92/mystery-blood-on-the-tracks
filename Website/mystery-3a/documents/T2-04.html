🔵 TIER 2 DOCUMENT — Technical analysis of the North Tower camera system failure on the night of July 18–19, 2025. This document covers the cause of the malfunction, timing, scope, and the six cameras that remained active. Players: examine what the surviving cameras did — and did not — capture.
I. Scope of Camera Failure

The Gran Horizonte Resort & Spa operates 94 cameras across the property: 67 resort security cameras (managed by Resort Security, Hikvision DS-9000 series) and 27 production cameras (managed by Stellar Content Studios, operated through an independent AV server in Room B1-AV). Both systems experienced partial failure on the night of July 18–19, 2025.

The 38 cameras that went offline are exclusively concentrated in the North Tower zone — the zone containing: the rooftop infinity pool (murder location), the production stairwell used by Victor Delgado, the North Tower contestant suites corridor, and the B1 basement corridor leading to Equipment Room B1. The south wing, lobby, restaurant, and exterior cameras remained fully operational.

II. Technical Cause of Failure

FGE-QR technical analysts examined the AV Server (Room B1-AV) logs retrieved from the Stellar Content Studios production tablet backup and the Gran Horizonte SCADA (Building Automation System) event log.

[B1-AV SERVER EVENT LOG — July 18, 2025] 22:02:07 CST — AV_SERVER_B1: CIRCUIT_LOAD_ANOMALY detected on Node 04 (North Tower feeds) 22:02:09 CST — AV_SERVER_B1: THERMAL_PROTECTION triggered — Node 04 load 187% nominal 22:02:11 CST — AV_SERVER_B1: FAILSAFE_MODE engaged — Node 04 cameras suspended (38 units) 22:02:11 CST — SYSTEM_NOTE: Manual override from device ID: PRD-TAB-001 (Production Admin Tablet) — Override timestamp: 21:58:44 CST (logged before event — pre-staged) 22:02:12 CST — Camera groups NT-A through NT-J: OFFLINE [END RELEVANT LOG SEGMENT]

The critical finding is in line 4: the system event log records a manual override command from device PRD-TAB-001 (the Production Admin Tablet) with a timestamp of 21:58:44 — approximately 3 minutes and 23 seconds before the malfunction event. This override was pre-staged: it was designed to trigger the failsafe when the server load reached threshold.

The production tablet (PRD-TAB-001) has admin credentials shared by two individuals: Vivian Fong (Associate Producer) and Victor Delgado (Show Host / Senior Production Staff). The executive producer's credentials are remote-only and were not active during this window.

III. Could the Malfunction Be Accidental?

Technical analysts examined whether the circuit overload could have been caused by normal production load. The North Tower camera system had been running at consistent load levels (63–71% nominal) for 18 consecutive days of production. Load records from the 30 days prior show no prior thermal protection events. The specific server node affected (Node 04) covers exclusively North Tower feeds — a deliberate or accidental overload on this node would require either a dramatic surge in camera activity (no production events were scheduled after 10 PM) or an externally introduced load spike.

Analyst Conclusion: The combination of the pre-staged override command (logged 3 minutes before the event), the specific targeting of the North Tower node, and the absence of any legitimate production reason for a 10 PM surge is inconsistent with accidental failure. This malfunction was deliberately induced.

The person who staged the camera override did so knowing it would create a blind zone at a specific location and a specific time — the North Tower rooftop, where Solomon Vance would be dead within 90 minutes.
