Discovery Account

How the Body Was Found

At approximately 01:10 AM, night pool attendant Aurelio Vásquez Gutiérrez began his scheduled perimeter check of the Main Pool (Zone B). Zone B has been under an exclusive production agreement with Solara Entertainment Group since July 1, 2025, which restricts access to production-credentialed individuals only after 10:00 PM. Mr. Vásquez Gutiérrez holds a production-issued access credential for maintenance purposes.

Upon approaching the pool's north edge at approximately 01:15 AM, Mr. Vásquez Gutiérrez observed a male individual floating face-down in the water, motionless, in the northeast section of the pool. He immediately called out to the individual and, receiving no response, retrieved the poolside emergency flotation device and attempted to reach the individual from the pool deck.

Before entering the water, Mr. Vásquez Gutiérrez noticed a hair dryer and a coiled orange extension cord on the pool deck approximately two meters from the northeast corner. The hair dryer was not plugged in at the moment of discovery — the cord had been disconnected from the outlet, which is located behind the pool bar counter. Mr. Vásquez Gutiérrez did not handle the item.

He contacted resort security via radio at 01:17 AM. Security Chief Camacho Reyes arrived at the pool within four minutes. Both attempted CPR on the extracted individual until emergency services arrived. The individual was pronounced dead at the scene by paramedics at 01:44 AM.

Aurelio Vásquez Gutiérrez — Night Pool Attendant · Initial Statement, 01:35 AM

"I saw him in the water and I thought he was sleeping at first. Then I saw his arms were wrong — spread out. And I saw the cord on the deck. I didn't touch anything. I just called security and tried to reach him with the float."

Scene Conditions

State of the Pool Area at Discovery

Security Chief Camacho Reyes documented the following conditions upon arrival at Zone B at approximately 01:21 AM:

Lighting Pool underwater lights active. Deck perimeter lights at 40% (overnight mode). Sufficient visibility to clearly observe the pool and surrounding deck.
Pool Area Single individual (the deceased) found face-down in the northeast section of the main pool. No other individuals present in Zone B. Pool water undisturbed aside from the body's position.
Equipment One (1) hair dryer (black, production-branded, model unidentified at time of report) and one (1) orange extension cord found on the pool deck, northeast corner. Cord disconnected from outlet. Hair dryer power switch in the ON position.
Electrical The GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) safety outlet behind the pool bar — the only outdoor outlet in Zone B — was found in a manually disabled state. The GFCI requires intentional physical action to bypass. Resort maintenance has confirmed this circuit was functioning correctly during a routine inspection on July 17, 2025.
Cameras Security confirmed that cameras C-7 and C-8, which cover the Zone B pool deck and terrace entry, were offline. The system showed both cameras in an offline status from approximately 22:15 until approximately 01:00 AM — at which point they resumed normal operation. No recordings exist for this period.
Beverages One (1) empty glass (rocks glass, containing residue consistent with rum or whiskey) found on the pool deck's north edge, approximately 1.5 meters from the body's pool entry point. One (1) second glass, partially consumed, on the pool bar counter.
Physical Evidence Visible burn marks on the deceased's left hand and left forearm, noted by Security Chief Camacho Reyes and confirmed by paramedics. Consistent with electrical contact injury, per paramedic observation.
