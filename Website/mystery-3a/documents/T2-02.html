🔵 TIER 2 DOCUMENT — Formal interview transcripts for all six persons of interest. Conducted in the early hours of July 19, 2025. Transcripts are summarized; full audio recordings are in FGE-QR custody.
Kavya Nair
Age 29 · UX Designer · Contestant | Interview: 02:35 AM · Room 1108 · Duration: 22 min
Cooperative · Emotional but consistent
Legal advisor: Not requested
Q [Montoya-Reyes]: Ms. Nair, what was your relationship with Solomon Vance?
"The show matched us at 94.7%. I thought that meant something. Then on Day 12, he said in front of everyone that it was a mistake. That the algorithm was wrong about him feeling anything for me. It was on camera. It's already circulating online. I trusted the system and he turned it into a spectacle."
Q: After the Day 12 incident, did you have any private conversations with Solomon?
"One. About two days later. I told him what he did was cruel and unnecessary. He said, 'You knew what you signed up for.' That was it. I didn't speak to him again after that."
Q: Where were you between 10:00 PM and 1:00 AM last night?
"Dinner until about 12:20. I left early. Then I called my sister from my room — I do that when I'm upset. The call went almost half an hour. Then I heard the security alert and stayed in my room."
Q: Did anyone visit your room after dinner?
"Zara came by sometime after midnight. We talked for a while. Maybe until one. She can tell you."
Q: Did you know anything about Solomon's activities beyond what was shown on camera?
[Pause] "...I saw him coming out of Zara's suite around 2 AM earlier in the week. I didn't say anything. I didn't want to be the person who causes drama."
ANALYST NOTE: Kavya disclosed the 2 AM observation of Solomon leaving Zara's suite unprompted at the end of the interview, after a pause. This detail corroborates the secret Solomon-Zara connection referenced in other accounts. Her phone records for the outgoing call at 12:31 AM to a San Jose, CA number have been subpoenaed and will corroborate her stated timeline.
Dominic Wallace
Age 31 · Physical Trainer · Contestant | Interview: 03:00 AM · Room 1106 · Duration: 31 min
Cooperative · Direct · Forthcoming
Legal advisor: Not requested
Q: You made a statement to Solomon Vance at 9:47 PM that was captured on camera. Can you explain what you said?
"I said he was a snake and that he was dead to me. Yes. I meant it. What he was doing with Zara — she's my match, she's a good person — he was playing with her while she thought they were just talking. I found out earlier that day. I was angry. I said it and I walked away."
Q: Did you physically touch Solomon at any point last night?
"No. Not once. I put my hands up when I walked away — I made sure to keep space. I knew cameras were on. I wasn't going to let him turn it into something physical."
Q: Where were you from 10:35 PM onwards?
"Las Brisas the whole night. I ate, I had drinks, I talked to Marcus [another contestant], to Zara for a bit. I was there until close to 1 AM. Ask the bartender — I had three Modelos and a water, he'll remember."
Q: How did you find out about Solomon and Zara?
"Rafael mentioned it, actually. Off-hand, like he was just noting it. He said he'd seen them together. I don't know how he knew."
ANALYST NOTE: Dominic's alibi at Las Brisas is confirmed by three witnesses interviewed separately and internal restaurant camera footage at 23:52. He left the restaurant at 00:40. The hallway camera at Floor 11 shows him entering his room at 00:44. He cannot have committed the murder. Importantly: he identifies Rafael Rios as the source of his information about Solomon-Zara — a detail Rafael has not volunteered.
Vivian Fong
Age 27 · Associate Producer · Production Staff | Interview: 03:40 AM · Conference Room G-02 · Duration: 38 min
Cooperative · Guarded · Legal advisor: not requested but asked about "company counsel" twice
Q: Walk me through your evening beginning at 10:00 PM.
"I got an alert that the camera system was down at 10:02. I went straight to Control Room B — that's my responsibility. I was there until security told me what happened. The whole night. I was working."
Q: What caused the camera malfunction?
"I — I'm still not fully sure. The AV server in B1 showed a circuit overload event. It happens. These systems are run hard during production. I was trying to diagnose it remotely but I couldn't reset it until the technician came in physically."
Q: Is it possible the circuit overload was triggered remotely, deliberately?
[Long pause] "Theoretically... the production tablet has access to server configuration. But you'd have to know what you were doing."
Q: Who has production tablet access?
"Me. Victor. The executive producer, but she's in LA. That's the full list with full admin rights."
Q: Did Solomon Vance ever approach you about the show's data or the algorithm?
[Visibly tense] "He was curious. Like I said in my first statement — he asked about how EROS works. I told him it was proprietary. He said something about already having looked at some data. I reported it to Victor."
Q: You reported it to Victor Delgado? Not to the executive producer?
"Victor is on-site. She's not. Victor said he'd handle it."
🔴 RED FLAG: Vivian reported Solomon's data inquiry directly to Victor Delgado — not to the executive producer or Solara Entertainment Group legal. Victor told her he would "handle it." She did not follow up. This makes Victor aware of Solomon's knowledge of the algorithm fraud at least one week before the murder. Cross-reference with Tier 3 documents.
Rafael Rios
Age 35 · Architect · Contestant | Interview: 04:10 AM · Room 1104 · Duration: 19 min
Minimally cooperative · Evasive on key points
Legal advisor: Present (contacted own attorney, Mexico City)
Q: When did you last speak with Solomon Vance?
"A few days ago. In passing."
Q: We have two witnesses who observed a conversation between you and Solomon in the corridor on Day 16 that appeared tense. What was discussed?
"Solomon was the kind of person who liked to... make observations. I don't think what we discussed is relevant to your investigation."
Q: I'm determining relevance, not you. What did he say?
[Consultation with attorney] "He made a comment about... assumptions. About my personal life. It was intrusive. I told him I didn't appreciate it. That was the end of it."
Q: Did Solomon ask for anything in exchange for his silence?
"I'd like to speak with my attorney before answering further questions."
ANALYST NOTE: Rafael's refusal to answer the direct question about whether Solomon demanded anything is notable. His attorney — reached remotely — has since advised him to cooperate fully but to decline to answer questions that may be self-incriminating under Mexican law. Rafael's keycard log confirms he was in his room from 00:14 AM. However, the murder window extends to 12:15 AM, and Rafael left dinner at 12:10 AM. His alibi for the final 4 minutes before room entry is unverified.
Zara Mirza
Age 30 · OB/GYN Resident · Contestant | Interview: 04:40 AM · Conference Room G-02 · Duration: 27 min
Fully cooperative · Precise · Self-composed
Legal advisor: Not requested
Q: Tell me about your conflict with Solomon Vance.
"He spread a lie about my health to multiple crew members. A medical lie. I confronted him. He denied it and said I couldn't prove it. I filed a complaint with the production liaison. Nothing happened. I was angry, yes. But I'm a physician. I know the difference between anger and action."
Q: Were you and Solomon ever more than casually acquainted?
[Brief pause] "We talked. More than was strictly related to the show, yes. I won't go into detail. That's private. It doesn't have anything to do with what happened to him."
Q: Where were you between midnight and 1 AM?
"I left dinner a little after midnight. I went to Kavya's room — I wanted to check on her. She's been having a rough time. We talked for maybe 45 minutes. Then I went back to my room."
Q: Kavya Nair's room is on Floor 11. Your room is also Floor 11. The rooftop pool where Solomon was found is Floor 15. What route did you take from Las Brisas to Floor 11?
"Main elevator. I didn't go to the roof. I have no reason to go to the roof."
ANALYST NOTE: Zara's account is internally consistent and corroborated by Kavya. Her timeline does not place her near the rooftop pool. Her medical background means she understands the mechanism of electrocution — but knowledge does not equal opportunity. Her alibi is the strongest among the five contestant-suspects and is corroborated by an independent witness.
Victor Delgado
Age 42 · Television Host · Show Staff | Interview: 05:20 AM · Conference Room G-02 · Duration: 41 min
Cooperative · Charming · Controlled
Legal advisor: Not requested ("nothing to hide")
Q: What time did you enter Production Suite B last night?
"Around ten o'clock. Maybe 10:10. The cameras were already having issues and I wanted to start reviewing what we had from the day."
Q: The hallway camera outside Production Suite B shows you entering at 12:47 AM — not 10:10 PM. Where were you between 10:10 PM and 12:47 AM?
[Pause. Maintained eye contact.] "I... may have stepped out a few times. I sometimes take walks to decompress during long production nights. I honestly don't remember specifically where I went."
Q: Your keycard accessed Equipment Room B1 at 10:22 PM and again at 11:47 PM. What were you doing there?
[First visible hesitation of the interview] "...I was looking for a cable adapter. We sometimes need adapters from that room. I may have gone twice."
Q: Was there a work order for that? Did you log the equipment retrieval?
"Not always — it's informal for small items. Ask Vivian, she'll confirm."
Q: Did you have any private conversations with Solomon Vance in the past week?
"Nothing beyond production check-ins."
Q: Vivian Fong told us that she reported Solomon's inquiry about the EROS algorithm data to you, and that you told her you would "handle it." Is that accurate?
[Long pause.] "Vivian was concerned. I told her I'd talk to him. These things come up with contestants — they get curious. I had a brief word with him. That was it."
🔴 RED FLAG: Victor's alibi has collapsed. He cannot account for his location between 10:10 PM and 12:47 AM — the exact window covering the camera malfunction, both equipment room accesses, and the estimated time of murder. His explanation for the equipment room entries ("cable adapter") is unverified, logged in no work order, and contradicts normal protocol. His confirmation that he spoke privately with Solomon about the algorithm data directly contradicts his earlier statement that their interactions were limited to "production schedule requirements." Victor Delgado is now the primary person of interest.
