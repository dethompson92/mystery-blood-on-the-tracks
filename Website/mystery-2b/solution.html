The Killer: Thomas Harrison

Title: Pullman Porter

The Motive

The 1957 investigation missed it entirely because of racial prejudice and class assumptions. Marchand was trafficking stolen artifacts recovered from WWII Europe. Thomas Harrison's family had been robbed of a priceless heirloom during the war. Thomas tracked the piece to Marchand, joined the rail staff specifically for this route, and intended to steal it back.

How It Happened

When Thomas entered Cabin A to retrieve the heirloom using his master key, Marchand woke up. A struggle ensued, and Thomas struck him with a heavy brass lamp. The blood on Thomas's cuffs, which the 1957 police dismissed as coming from him "checking for a pulse," was proven by 2024 blood spatter re-analysis to be high-velocity impact spatter, definitively placing him holding the weapon when the blow was struck.
