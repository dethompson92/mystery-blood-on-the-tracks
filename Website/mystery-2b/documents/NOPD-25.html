INVESTIGATOR'S PREFACE:

This timeline reconstructs the events aboard the Silver Comet train (Seaboard Air Line Railroad) from the evening of November 13th to the morning of November 14th, 1957. Data compiled from witness statements (Washington, DeLuca, Sinclair, Whitfield, et al.), conductor logs, and forensic estimates.

CRITICAL NOTE: Time of death is estimated between 02:45 and 03:00 AM based on stopped watch (03:17) and rigor mortis analysis. All passenger alibis must be verified against this specific window.

DETECTIVE'S ANALYSIS & DISCREPANCIES:
  • LOCKED ROOM ISSUE: Door was locked from INSIDE. Killer must have had way to lock from outside OR victim locked it after killer left (impossible given death) OR killer is someone victim let in/out.
  • DELUCA ALIBI: Vincent DeLuca has solid alibi for 01:45-04:00. Unless bartender is lying, DeLuca CANNOT be the killer, despite motive/argument.
  • MISSING TIME: 02:45-03:00 is the critical window. Who was awake?
    • Whitfield (heard voices)
    • Sinclair (heard door)
    • Devereaux (light under door at 03:10)
  • THE MYSTERY WOMAN: Sinclair's sighting of "woman in burgundy" at 22:45 is crucial. Matches description of Devereaux, but Devereaux claims not to know Marchand. If they were talking at 22:45, Devereaux is lying.
  • FOOTSTEPS: 02:50 AM footsteps + 02:55 AM door click + 03:10 AM light in Comp 8 suggests killer exited Comp 7, walked short distance, entered Comp 8. Distance between Comp 7 and Comp 8 is approx 4 feet.
