🔍 MYSTERY #2B: BLOOD ON THE TRACKS

Investigation Guide for Players

Welcome, Detective!

You are about to investigate one of the most perplexing locked-room mysteries in American criminal history. On November 14, 1957, jazz singer Vivienne Marchand was murdered aboard the luxurious Silver Comet passenger train. The crime remained unsolved for 67 years—until new evidence emerged in 2024.

Your mission: Examine all the evidence, interview transcripts, and forensic reports to determine: WHO killed Vivienne Marchand, HOW they did it, and WHY?

📋 How to Play

  1. Read the Documents: You have access to 20+ original police reports, witness statements, forensic analyses, and investigative notes from both 1957 and 2024.
  2. Analyze the Evidence: Look for inconsistencies, hidden connections, and overlooked clues.
  3. Identify the Killer: Determine which passenger aboard the train committed murder.
  4. Explain the Method: Figure out how the killer escaped from a locked compartment.
  5. Understand the Motive: Discover why this seemingly motiveless crime occurred.
💡 Tip: This is a HISTORICAL mystery with sensitive themes. The case involves LGBTQ+ persecution in 1957 America. Approach with empathy and historical awareness.

🎯 Key Questions to Answer

The Core Mystery:

  1. WHO is the killer? Which passenger on the train strangled Vivienne Marchand?
  2. HOW did the killer escape from a compartment that was locked from the inside?
  3. WHY was Vivienne murdered? What was the motive?
  4. WHAT happened to the murder weapon (the burgundy-gold silk scarf)?
  5. WHO is the "mystery woman in burgundy" that witness Margaret Sinclair saw?

🕵️ Persons of Interest

Pay Close Attention To:

  • The Mystery Woman in Burgundy: Seen talking with victim at 10:45 PM—never identified by 1957 investigators
  • Vincent DeLuca: Victim's fiancé, traveling in separate car
  • Dr. Evelyn Hayes: Physician in corridor during estimated time of murder
  • Margaret Sinclair: Witness who saw the mystery woman
  • Charlotte Devereaux: Passenger in Compartment 2 (read 2024 documents for more)

🔬 Critical Evidence

Evidence That Will Solve the Case:

  • Victim's watch stopped at 3:17 AM (time of death?)
  • Door locked from INSIDE when body discovered
  • Missing burgundy-gold silk scarf (murder weapon)
  • Ligature marks showing fabric weave pattern on victim's neck
  • No signs of forced entry or struggle
  • Witness testimony about "woman in burgundy" talking with victim
  • 2024 discovery: Trunk containing scarf, journal, love letters
💡 Advanced Tip: The 1957 investigators couldn't solve this case because they didn't understand the relationship between victim and killer. The 2024 documents reveal what was hidden. Compare 1957 witness statements with 2024 confession carefully!
