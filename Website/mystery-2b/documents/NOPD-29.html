GAMEPLAY FACILITATION GUIDE
Mystery #2B: "Blood on the Tracks" (1957)

Comprehensive Instructor's Manual for Educators & Game Masters

I. OVERVIEW FOR FACILITATORS

Objective: Guide participants through a dual-timeline investigation (1957 & 2024) to solve the murder of Vivienne Marchand, while exploring themes of forensic science evolution and historical LGBTQ+ persecution.
Parameter Details
Difficulty Level 3/5 (Medium) INTERMEDIATE
Estimated Time 2.5 - 3 Hours (Full Investigation & Discussion)
Recommended Age 16+ (Mature themes: murder, homophobia, discrimination)
Group Size 1-8 Players (Individual or Collaborative Team)
Required Materials Documents #01 - #23 (Player Set)
NOTE: Withhold Document #27 (Solution)

II. CONTENT WARNINGS & PREPARATION

⚠️ CRITICAL DISCUSSION POINTS

Before beginning, facilitators MUST address the sensitive nature of this case:

  • LGBTQ+ Persecution: The core motive involves the "Lavender Scare" era (1950s) where homosexuality was criminalized.
  • Violence: The case details a murder by strangulation.
  • Historical Discrimination: Documents reflect 1957 attitudes toward race and sexuality.
  • Emotional Safety: This content may be triggering or difficult for some participants.

Recommended Introduction: Begin with a 10-minute presentation using Document #28 (Historical Context) to set the stage. Explain that understanding the social climate of 1957 is as important as analyzing the physical evidence.

III. SETUP & MATERIALS DISTRIBUTION

Phase 1: Introduction (15 Minutes)

  1. Distribute Document #23 (Player Investigation Guide). Allow players to read the overview.
  2. Explain the Structure: "You are investigating a cold case. You have access to original 1957 police files AND modern 2024 forensic reports."
  3. Set Ground Rules: Emphasize respectful language when discussing historical prejudice.

Phase 2: Case File Distribution (10 Minutes)

Choose the method that fits your group:

  • Option A: Full Release (Experienced Groups): Provide Documents #01-#23 simultaneously. Players must sort and prioritize.
  • Option B: Staged Release (Recommended for Classrooms):
    • Start: Docs #01-#06 (The 1957 Crime Scene)
    • +45 Mins: Docs #07-#11 (The 2024 Breakthrough)
    • +90 Mins: Docs #12-#18 (Deep Dive Witness Statements)
    • +2 Hrs: Docs #19-#22 (Resolution - if stuck)
  • Option C: Evidence Board: Print documents and tack them to a wall. Players physically move to read them, simulating a real detective squad room.

IV. FACILITATION STRATEGIES

Group Investigation Tips

  • Assign Roles: Lead Detective, Evidence Analyst, Timeline Keeper, Scribe.
  • Visualize: Provide large butcher paper for constructing a timeline.
  • Hypothesis Tracking: Use sticky notes to map relationships between suspects.

Difficulty Modifications

EASIER VERSION HARDER VERSION

V. KEY TEACHING MOMENTS

Use these pause points to deepen critical thinking:

AFTER DOCUMENT #15 (Margaret Sinclair's Statement):

ASK: "Who is the mystery woman? Why couldn't 1957 investigators identify her?"

TEACHING POINT: Social invisibility. Two women talking wasn't seen as suspicious or romantic, so investigators didn't probe that relationship.

AFTER DOCUMENTS #09-#11 (Journal & Letters):

ASK: "Why did Charlotte hide these for 67 years? What would have happened if they were found in 1957?"

TEACHING POINT: The Lavender Scare. Possession of these items was dangerous—potentially evidence of a felony (sodomy) and grounds for social ruin.

VI. EXPECTED PROGRESSION & HINTS

Typical Solving Path

  1. Establish Time of Death (2:45-3:00 AM) via medical report.
  2. Identify suspects in Car 7 who had opportunity.
  3. Note the Missing Scarf is the murder weapon.
  4. Connect the "Mystery Woman" (Doc #15) to the passenger list.
  5. Eliminate Vincent DeLuca via alibi (Doc #12 - Bartender).
  6. Focus on Charlotte Devereaux (Adjacent compartment, right time).
  7. Use 2024 Evidence to reveal the hidden motive.
  8. Confirm with Confession (Doc #20).
HINT SYSTEM (Give in order if stuck):
1. "What do we know about the missing scarf? Who last had it?"
2. "Re-read Margaret Sinclair's description of the woman in burgundy. Check the passenger list."
3. "Why would someone hide evidence for 67 years? What secret is worth that effort?"
4. "Compare Charlotte's 1957 statement (Doc #10) with the 2024 discovery. What did she lie about?"
5. "The key is a relationship that NO ONE in 1957 would have suspected."

VII. POST-INVESTIGATION DISCUSSION

Use these questions for a 60-90 minute debriefing session.

Forensic Science

  • How did the lack of DNA technology in 1957 allow the killer to escape?
  • What specific modern test solved the case? (Touch DNA on scarf).

Social History & Ethics

  • Did the social stigma against homosexuality contribute to the murder itself? (Fear of exposure was the motive).
  • Is it ethical to "out" Charlotte and Vivienne 67 years later?
  • How does historical context change our view of the killer? (Villain vs. Victim of circumstance).

VIII. ASSESSMENT OPTIONS

Written Assignments:

  • Essay: "The Impact of the Lavender Scare on Criminal Justice."
  • Investigative Report: Summarize the evidence as if writing the final police file.
  • Character Profile: Psychological analysis of Charlotte Devereaux.

Creative Projects:

  • Record a "True Crime Podcast" episode about the case.
  • Write a newspaper article from 1957 vs. 2024 perspectives.

X. TROUBLESHOOTING

"Players miss critical clues":

  • Gently redirect: "Let's look closely at Document #15 again."
  • Ask questions, don't give answers. "Who else was in the hallway?"

"Uncomfortable discussions":

  • Remind group of ground rules.
  • Reframe: "We are observing history, not judging it by modern standards, but we must acknowledge the harm."
