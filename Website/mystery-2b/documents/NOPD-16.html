PHOTOGRAPH #3: VICTIM - CLOSE-UP OF NECK AND FACE
[PHOTOGRAPH PLACEHOLDER]
CLOSE-UP: NECK TRAUMA
⚠️ GRAPHIC FORENSIC DETAIL

Evidence ID: JPD-57-1114-003

Description: Close-up forensic detail of victim's neck and lower face. Clear evidence of ligature strangulation: horizontal patterned mark circling throat, approximately 1.5 inches wide, with distinct fabric weave impression. Bruising and discoloration consistent with compression of carotid arteries and jugular veins. Petechial hemorrhaging visible in eyes and surrounding facial tissue.

Key Observations: Ligature mark shows fabric texture pattern—appears consistent with silk or fine woven material. Mark is relatively even in depth around circumference, suggesting sustained pressure rather than violent thrashing. No finger marks or hand-shaped bruising—rules out manual strangulation. Victim's expression appears peaceful, eyes closed, mouth slightly parted—supports theory of attack during sleep.

Forensic Significance: The width and pattern of the ligature mark will be critical for identifying the murder weapon. Medical examiner noted that the fabric impression suggests a decorative scarf or similar textile item, approximately 2-3 feet in length when used.

Camera Settings: f/16, 1/15 sec, Flash with diffusion | Taken: 11/14/1957, 07:45 hrs

PHOTOGRAPH #5: COMPARTMENT DOOR AND LOCK MECHANISM
[PHOTOGRAPH PLACEHOLDER]
EVIDENCE: DOOR LOCK MECHANISM
INTERIOR VIEW

Evidence ID: JPD-57-1114-005

Description: Interior view of compartment door showing lock mechanism in "engaged" position. Standard railroad compartment lock with manual deadbolt (brass) and privacy latch (steel). Both locks were found engaged from interior side, as reported by porter who used master key to gain entry. No signs of forced entry, tampering, or damage to lock mechanism or door frame.

Key Observations: Door was definitively locked from the INSIDE when porter attempted entry. This creates significant investigative paradox: How did perpetrator exit compartment after committing murder? Possible scenarios: (1) Victim locked door after perpetrator departed, then was later attacked by someone with master key access; (2) Perpetrator had ability to manipulate lock from outside; (3) Attack occurred before victim secured door for night, and door was locked post-mortem by perpetrator to delay discovery.

Porter's Statement Reference: "I knocked three times for the 6:30 wake-up call Miss Marchand had requested. When I received no answer, I used my master key. Both the deadbolt and privacy latch were engaged from inside. I had to use significant force to turn the mechanism."

Camera Settings: f/11, 1/30 sec, Flash | Taken: 11/14/1957, 08:05 hrs
