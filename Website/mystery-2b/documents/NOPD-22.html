Cold Case Unit - Final Case Closure Report

✓ CASE SOLVED AFTER 67 YEARS

Final Status: Case NOPD-COLD-2024-0847

Original Case: JPD 57-1114-HOMI (Jackson, Mississippi, 1957)

Victim: Vivienne Marchand (DOB: 8/12/1925, DOD: 11/14/1957)

Perpetrator: Charlotte "Lottie" Devereaux (DOB: 3/17/1929, DOD: 11/24/2024)

Original Case Status: Unsolved (1957-2024 / 67 years inactive)

Final Case Status: SOLVED - CLOSED BY CONFESSION (November 18, 2024)

Resolution Summary:

On November 3, 2024, a locked trunk was discovered in Charlotte Devereaux's estate containing the murder weapon (victim's silk scarf with DNA evidence), personal journals documenting motive, love letters establishing romantic relationship between suspect and victim, and suspect's train ticket placing her at crime scene.

On November 18, 2024, Charlotte Devereaux provided full voluntary confession to NOPD Cold Case detectives, admitting to strangling Vivienne Marchand aboard the Silver Comet train on November 14, 1957. Confession was corroborated by physical and documentary evidence.

Motive: Betrayal and fear of exposure. Devereaux and Marchand were secret romantic partners 1954-1957. When Marchand became engaged to a man, Devereaux feared losing her and potential exposure of their illegal same-sex relationship.

Method: Ligature strangulation using victim's silk scarf while victim slept. Devereaux had been invited into victim's compartment earlier that evening. She returned at approximately 3:17 AM and committed murder.

Why Case Went Unsolved (1957): Relationship between victim and suspect was completely secret. No one knew to connect them. Devereaux presented herself as ordinary passenger with no knowledge of victim. In 1957, investigators would not have imagined secret lesbian relationship as motive.

Justice Achieved: While criminal prosecution is impossible due to suspect's death, historical truth has been established. Victim's memory is honored. Cold case is definitively solved.

Family Notification: Victim's distant relatives were located and notified. They expressed gratitude for closure after 67 years.

Public Statement: NOPD and Orleans Parish DA issued joint statement on December 1, 2024, acknowledging solution of historic cold case and recognizing historical context of LGBTQ+ persecution in mid-20th century America.
