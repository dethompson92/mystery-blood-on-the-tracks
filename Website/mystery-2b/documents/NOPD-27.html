III. WHY THE CASE WENT COLD (1957)

The 1957 investigation failed due to a combination of social prejudice, missing evidence, and effective deception:

  1. The Invisible Motive: In 1957, a lesbian relationship was socially invisible. Investigators did not consider a "lover's quarrel" between two women. They looked for a male killer.
  2. Missing Weapon: Charlotte took the scarf. Without the weapon, there was no physical link to the killer.
  3. Technological Limits: No DNA testing existed. Fingerprints on the door were inconclusive.
  4. The Red Herring: Vincent DeLuca (jealous fiancé) fit the profile perfectly. His presence distracted police, even though he had a solid alibi.
  5. "The Mystery Woman": Witness Margaret Sinclair saw Charlotte but didn't know her name. Police never connected the "woman in burgundy" to the passenger in Compartment 8.

IV. HOW THE 2024 BREAKTHROUGH OCCURRED

The Turning Point: The discovery of Charlotte Devereaux's locked trunk in November 2024.
  • Physical Evidence: The missing burgundy scarf was in the trunk.
  • Forensic Link: Modern DNA analysis found Vivienne's skin cells embedded in the scarf fabric (impossible to detect in 1957).
  • Documentary Proof: Charlotte's journal details the affair and her guilt. Love letters confirm the relationship.
  • Confession: Charlotte left a written confession days before her death at age 95.

VIII. HISTORICAL CONTEXT

The Lavender Scare: In the 1950s, the US government actively persecuted LGBTQ+ people, firing thousands from jobs. Homosexuality was illegal and classified as a mental illness. Exposure meant total ruin. This context is essential to understanding why Charlotte was so terrified of Vivienne exposing her, and why investigators never suspected a romantic link between two women.
