01 — System Overview

How the Checkpoint System Works

Mystery 3A uses a three-objective progressive evidence system inspired by Unsolved Case Files' sealed-envelope mechanic, adapted for digital delivery. Players begin with a full initial evidence package (Tier 1). Each time they correctly answer an objective, the next evidence tier unlocks automatically in the website UI.

The system is designed for Easy difficulty — clues are unambiguous, the timeline is clear, and each objective builds logically on the last. The architecture supports future complexity scaling: additional red herrings, false forensic reports, or branching suspect paths can be added to create Medium and Hard variants.

02 — Objectives & Official Answers

The Three Objectives

Objective 01 — Tier 1
Confirm the Cause & Manner of Death
Initial Package
Player Question
"Based on the evidence available, how did Solomon Vance die? Was this an accident, suicide, or homicide — and what was the instrument of death?"
✅ Official Answer (Acceptable Variations)

Solomon Vance was electrocuted. A hotel hair dryer was dropped (or thrown) into the resort swimming pool while he was in the water. The manner of death is homicide — the hair dryer was connected to power via an extension cord that bypassed the pool area's GFCI outlet. The damage was deliberate and required prior knowledge of the electrical system.

Checkpoint 2 Unlock

"You have confirmed the murder method and identified who had access and opportunity. Now examine the physical evidence. Who killed Solomon Vance — and why?"

Victor Delgado — show host and executive producer — killed Solomon Vance. His fingerprints were found on the extension cord connecting the hair dryer to the pool-area outlet. 04 — Solution (GM Only)

The Killer & Motive

⚠ Spoiler — Solution Package Content
Victor Delgado
Show Host & Executive Producer — Solara Entertainment Group
Victor Delgado's real name is not Victor Delgado. He fabricated his identity — credentials, production history, even his nationality — to secure the EP role at SEG. Solomon Vance, as a tech investor with industry connections, discovered the discrepancy through a background database he had access to via his investment firm. Solomon arranged a private meeting to confront Victor, giving Victor 48 hours to come clean or be exposed. Victor, facing the collapse of his career and the $50M Series B franchise deal he stood to personally profit from, chose elimination. He used his executive access to the production infrastructure — knowledge no contestant would have — to construct the perfect crime scene during the camera blackout he himself created.
🎣 Red Herring — Dominic Wallace

Dominic Wallace (Suspect 2) is the intended red herring. He publicly threatened Solomon at 9:45 PM in front of multiple witnesses, giving players the most obvious suspect with apparent motive. However, Dominic has an unbreakable alibi: he was at the cast dinner from 10:00 PM until 12:30 AM, confirmed by 5 contestants, a production assistant, and dining room camera footage that was never offline. Players who focus on Dominic will find his alibi ironclad in the Tier 2 documents and must pivot to examining who actually had access to the equipment room.

05 — Quality Verification

Document Quality Checklist

Every document generated for Mystery 3A must pass this 10-point checklist before upload to AI Drive. A score of 9.5/10 or higher is required. Flag any failure and regenerate before proceeding.

  • ✅Name Consistency: All character names match the locked roster (Solomon Vance, Kavya Nair, Dominic Wallace, Vivian Fong, Rafael Rios, Zara Mirza, Victor Delgado). No original names used.
  • ✅Timeline Consistency: All timestamps align with the master timeline (camera off 22:15, equipment room 22:22, death ~00:05, cameras on 01:00, body found 01:15).
  • ✅No Spoilers in Tier 1: Tier 1 documents may not identify Victor as the killer, name the extension cord as the murder weapon's delivery system, or reveal the identity-fraud motive.
  • ✅Brand Consistency: Show documents use Forever Match branding. Investigative documents use FGE-QR header, bilingual formatting, IBM Plex Mono for technical data.
  • ✅Bilingual Toggle: All investigative docs (Mexican official) have Spanish primary text and English translation. Show documents are English-only.
  • ✅Cross-References: Each document cites at least one related document by filename/ID where relevant.
  • ✅Case File Number: FGE-QR-2025-07-0847 appears on all investigative documents.
  • ✅Tier Gating: Tier 2+ documents contain no information that should only appear in Tier 3. Solution package content appears only in the Solution Package.
  • ✅Printability: Each document has a print-mode CSS block that renders cleanly in black-and-white on standard letter/A4 paper.
  • ✅Narrative Accuracy: All factual claims in the document (forensic details, location names, dates, legal references) are internally consistent with the Story Bible (F1).
