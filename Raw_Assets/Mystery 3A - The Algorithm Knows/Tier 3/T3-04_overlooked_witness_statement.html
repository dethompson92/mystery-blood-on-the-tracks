II. Interview Transcript — July 23, 2025
OFFICIAL INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT — SUPPLEMENTAL
FGE-DS-2025-0847-08 · 14:30–15:22 hrs · Room 204, Gran Horizonte Resort
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: Rodrigo, thank you for coming back in. Can you start by describing your regular patrol route on the night of July 18th?
Yes. My shift starts at 10 PM. I check each floor from 11 to 14 on a 90-minute rotation — that means I pass each level roughly twice before the shift ends at 6 AM. The rooftop pool area I check at the top and bottom of each rotation. It's the highest-risk area for unauthorized guest access after midnight.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: And the rooftop pool — who had authorized access at that hour?
Officially, the rooftop closes to guests at 11 PM. After that, only maintenance staff with a keycard and production crew with special access cards can go up. The show — Forever Match — had a blanket production access authorization that covered certain staff. But guests, including the contestants, were not supposed to be up there after 11.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: Walk me through what you observed around midnight. Take your time.
I was coming up the stairwell from Floor 13 to the rooftop access door — that would have been around 11:50 PM, maybe 11:52. The door from the stairwell to the rooftop pool deck is a heavy fire door, and it was propped open maybe ten centimeters — that's unusual, we're supposed to keep it sealed. I pushed it slightly open and looked through.

I saw a man walking toward the pool from the far door — the service entrance near the equipment room side. He was carrying something in each hand — it was dark, the main pool lights were off, but there's ambient lighting from the city and the resort signage. He was moving quickly, purposefully. Not like someone out for a walk.
[Note: Espinoza-Vargas demonstrated the walking posture — brisk, slightly hunched forward, carrying items at his sides.]
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: Could you identify this person?
Not immediately. The lighting was low. But as he passed the southern edge of the pool — there's a strip light under the water-feature wall — I recognized his face. It was Victor Delgado. The show host. I know his face — he's on television and I'd seen him around the resort for a week. I was fairly certain. Maybe 90 percent.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: What was he carrying?
One item was a handheld device — like a pistol-shaped appliance, maybe 30 centimeters long. I thought at the time it could be a drill or a hair dryer. The other hand had what looked like coiled cable — like an extension cord, dark-colored. He was holding it over his shoulder by the loop.
[Note: Witness shown photograph of Conair 1875W hair dryer (Exhibit EX-006-HDD). Response: "Yes. That shape and size. That's consistent with what I saw."]
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: Did you see anyone else on the roof?
Not immediately. But as I was watching — I was still in the doorway, I hadn't announced myself — I saw another figure coming up the main stairwell door on the opposite side of the pool, about 30 seconds later. This person moved differently — more hesitant, slower. They stood at the far end of the pool. I couldn't see that person clearly at all. Just a silhouette.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: Why didn't you intervene or report this at the time?
I thought — this is a TV production. These people have authorization cards. I had seen crew up there late before setting up equipment for early-morning shoots. I assumed Señor Delgado was there in his professional capacity, maybe coordinating a surprise filming setup. The show does things like that. I made a note in my mental log but not in my physical patrol report because I didn't want to get someone from the show in trouble for something that was probably authorized. It wasn't until Officer Ríos mentioned the camera blackout and the equipment room that I realized what I had seen was different.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: What time did you leave the rooftop access area?
I stepped back from the door and continued my floor 14 corridor check. That would have been around 11:54 or 11:55 PM. I did not return to the rooftop until my next rotation at approximately 1:15 AM, when I found the body.
DRA. MONTOYA-REYES: To be clear — you found the body?
Yes. I reported it immediately. That is in the original incident report. But they never asked me what I had seen earlier that night. I should have said something then. I feel — I am sorry I didn't.
[Note: Witness visibly distressed at this point. Interview paused for 5 minutes.]
