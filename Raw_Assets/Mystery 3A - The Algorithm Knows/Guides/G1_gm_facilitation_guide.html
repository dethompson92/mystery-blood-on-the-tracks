Pre-Game Setup (15 min before players arrive)

Sort and organize documents in labeled Tier envelopes or folders. Players should never see Tier 2 or Tier 3 documents at the start. Do a quick read-through of T1-01 (Player Investigation Guide) so you can answer basic questions about how the system works.

Do not distribute the Story Bible (F1), Brand Guide (F2), Objective Framework (F3), Solution documents (S1, S2), or this guide (G1) to players at any time.
Print and laminate or use digital display for all player-facing documents. Dark background HTML files look excellent on tablets or laptops. Consider using a shared screen for dramatic reveals.

Running the Three Tiers
Tier 1 — Open Investigation (Free Evidence)
0 – 40 MIN

Distribute all T1 documents at the start. Players receive the case overview, police report, incident report, victim profile, show overview, and all six suspect profiles. This is the "surface layer" — everything the public investigation would know.

Key T1 discoveries to watch for: the 2-hour camera gap, the equipment room access anomaly, Victor's stated alibi, and the six suspects' relationship to Solomon.

"The FGE has completed its initial investigation. Here is everything gathered in the first 24 hours. Study it carefully — the truth is already in here, if you know where to look."
If players are struggling after 30 minutes, have "Investigadora Montoya-Reyes" call in (you, speaking in role) with a hint: "We've been looking at the keycard logs. One person accessed Equipment Room B1 twice in one night. That's unusual."
Tier 2 — Advanced Evidence (Earned Documents)
40 – 70 MIN

Release all five T2 documents together when players demonstrate engagement with the keycard access anomaly or camera blackout. This includes interview transcripts, forensics, camera analysis, and production communications.

Key T2 discoveries: Victor's alibi collapses (interview transcripts), Victor reported the camera malfunction he caused, and Vivian reports to Victor about the camera "glitch."

"The investigation has gone deeper. The FGE has obtained the full interview transcripts, technical camera analysis, and internal production communications. A pattern is emerging."
T2 communications show Vivian's fraud but not her guilt for murder. Players may over-focus on her. Let them explore it — the T3 algorithm audit will show her full role without implicating her in the killing.
Tier 3 — Definitive Evidence (Case-Closing)
70 – 90 MIN

Release all four T3 documents when players have a specific suspect in mind (even if wrong) or are ready to make accusations. T3 should feel like the dramatic final act — each document a nail in the coffin.

T3 documents in order of impact: Fingerprint → Deleted Texts → Algorithm Audit → Witness Statement. Consider revealing them one at a time for maximum dramatic effect.

"The FGE forensics lab has completed its full analysis. What you are about to read is the definitive evidence. Study it carefully before you make your final accusation."
For maximum drama: reveal T3-04 (the witness statement) last, after players have accused Victor. Let it serve as the crowning confirmation — the one piece of evidence that places him at the scene with the weapons in hand.
Frequently Asked GM Questions
What if players accuse Victor early (without T3 evidence)?
Accept the accusation and ask them to present their reasoning. If their case is sound (keycard + alibi gap + false identity), validate them and release T3 as confirmation. If their reasoning has gaps, give T3 to strengthen or test their theory.
What if players never suspect Victor and focus only on Vivian?
Release T3-01 (fingerprint) early — Vivian's prints are not on the cord. This eliminates her from direct murder involvement and redirects focus. You can also note in-character: "The FGE notes that Vivian Fong never accessed Equipment Room B1."
What if players want to know more about Solomon's past (SEC, Caymans)?
Keep it vague — Solomon is the victim, not the focus. You can say: "There are indications Solomon had financial complications in his past, but the FGE has determined they are not directly related to his death."
How do I handle players who want to "interview" suspects?
Use the T2-02 interview transcripts as the formal interview record. If players want to ask additional questions, you can improvise brief responses in-character using the story bible character sheets — but stay consistent with the documented facts.
What if a player has read ahead and already knows the solution?
Assign them the GM assistant role — they help manage documents and track clues without contributing their foreknowledge to the group's deduction process.
How explicit are the adult themes?
The player-facing documents reference mature themes (false STI rumor, closeted sexuality, blackmail) but are written tastefully. Nothing is gratuitous. The GM story bible has fuller detail but this stays GM-only. Read the room with your group and adjust framing as needed.
Atmosphere & Immersion Tips

Music: Play ambient electronic/pop music reminiscent of reality TV shows during the investigation phases. Switch to tense, minimal electronic music when releasing Tier 3 evidence. Silence for the final accusation and reveal.

Lighting: Dim or blue-filtered lighting adds atmosphere. If available, project the Forever Match logo or crime scene images on a screen behind you.

Character: Speak as "Investigadora Montoya-Reyes" when delivering in-universe information. Switch to your own voice as GM when explaining meta-game rules.

Pacing: The natural investigation arc is: curiosity → confusion → theories → suspects → evidence → conviction. Don't rush players through this arc. The confusion phase is where the best conversations happen.

Keep a physical or digital whiteboard where all players can see suspects and evidence. Visualizing connections dramatically accelerates the investigation and creates shared "aha" moments.
