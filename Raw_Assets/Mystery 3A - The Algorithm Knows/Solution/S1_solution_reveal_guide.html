Overview — How to Run the Reveal

The solution reveal is the emotional climax of the entire mystery experience. It should feel earned, dramatic, and satisfying — the moment where all the scattered evidence snaps into a single clear picture. This guide walks you through the reveal in five distinct phases, with scripted GM lines, pacing tips, and instructions for handling different player outcomes.

Total reveal runtime: 20–35 minutes depending on player discussion. Do not rush it.

Set the atmosphere first. Dim lights slightly, play the Forever Match theme music softly if available. Ask all players to put down their evidence documents and gather together. Say: "The investigation is now closed. It's time to hear what the FGE has concluded."
Pre-Reveal: Collect Accusations

Before beginning the reveal, ask each player (or team) to write down their accusation on a slip of paper:

  • • Accused: (suspect name)
  • • Murder Weapon: (what killed Solomon)
  • • Motive: (why they did it)

Collect the papers. Do not reveal them yet. This creates accountability and prevents players from changing their answer once the reveal begins.

The Five-Phase Reveal
1
The Hook — Remind Them What's at Stake
~3 MIN
📢 GM Script
"Solomon Vance came to this island looking for love. At least — that's what he told us. What he actually found was a man who knew his darkest secret. And on the night of July 18th, that man decided Solomon knew too much. The algorithm promised perfect matches. But the real match made that night was between a killer and his victim — and it was no accident."
Pause here. Let the atmosphere land. If players try to jump in with guesses, say: "All will be revealed. Let the FGE build its case."
2
The Elimination — Clearing the Innocents
~8 MIN

Walk through each innocent suspect. Acknowledge their secrets and motives — but show why each one is cleared. This validates player investigation and honors the depth of the red herrings.

A
Kavya Nair — Had motive (humiliation) but cast dinner alibi confirmed by 6 witnesses and security footage. She was protecting Solomon's secret, not ending him.
B
Dominic Wallace — Strongest emotional motive (betrayal). But solid alibi — never left cast dinner until 12:40 AM, confirmed multiple witnesses. His anger was real; his presence at the pool was impossible.
C
Vivian Fong — Career motive was strong. But she never left Camera Control Room B. Ironically, she was trying to cover up the EROS fraud — without realizing her boss was doing something far more extreme to protect the same secret.
D
Rafael Rios — Feared exposure of his sexuality. Significant motive. But keycard logs place him in his suite from 12:14 AM. He confronted Solomon verbally — he never considered violence.
E
Zara Mirza — Furious about the false STI rumor. Legitimate motive. But she was with Kavya in her suite until 1 AM — cross-confirmed alibis. Her confrontation with Solomon happened days earlier.
📢 GM Script — Transition
"Five suspects. Five people with reasons to want Solomon Vance gone. Five people who, on the night he died, were exactly where they said they were. All except one."
3
The Build — Victor's Evidence Cascade
~10 MIN

Present the evidence against Victor in mounting order — each piece building on the last. Pause after each item for effect.

1
The Double Keycard: Victor accessed Equipment Room B1 at 10:22 PM and again at 11:47 PM. Two entries. No maintenance work was scheduled. He retrieved the hair dryer and extension cord. The equipment was found with his fingerprints.
2
The Blind Spot: Victor manipulated the resort's camera system to create a 94-camera blackout from 10:02 PM to 12:47 AM — including every camera covering the rooftop pool. He used his production access to trigger it from Camera Control Panel A — the panel that Vivian was NOT at. He created the darkness he needed.
3
The Fingerprint: A partial left thumbprint on the extension cord plug — a gripping position consistent with insertion into a wall socket — matched Victor Delgado's elimination sample with 14 minutiae points. He was the last person to handle that cord before it killed Solomon Vance.
4
The Deleted Messages: Forensics recovered 47 deleted SMS messages between Victor and Solomon — a six-week blackmail chain. Victor demanded $5,000, then $25,000 per week in cryptocurrency. Solomon tried to fight back. The final message, sent at 8:15 PM on July 18th: "Come to the rooftop pool. Midnight. Alone. No cameras tonight — I've arranged it."
5
The Eyewitness: Security guard Rodrigo Espinoza-Vargas saw Victor Delgado on the rooftop at 11:52 PM — carrying a hair dryer and an extension cord — heading toward the pool. Victor's own alibi claimed he was in Production Suite B. He was not.
6
The Truth He Was Protecting: Victor Delgado's real name is Víctor Manuel Soto-Guerrero. In 2011, he was convicted of wire fraud and served time under a federal plea deal. He built a false identity to escape his past. Solomon Vance had discovered this — and had also discovered that the EROS algorithm was completely fake. Victor had two devastating secrets. Solomon knew both of them. Silence was the only option Victor could live with.
4
The Accusation — Name the Killer
~4 MIN
📢 GM Script — The Accusation
"On the night of July 18th, 2025, at approximately 12:05 AM, Victor Delgado — born Víctor Manuel Soto-Guerrero — carried a Conair 1875W hair dryer and an extension cord onto the rooftop pool deck of the Gran Horizonte Resort. He had arranged for the cameras to be blind. He had arranged for Solomon Vance to be there alone. And then he dropped that powered hair dryer into the pool. Solomon Vance was dead within seconds. Victor walked back to his suite, deleted every message on his phone, and in the morning, stood before investigators with a look of practiced grief on his face. The algorithm, he told the cameras, never lies. He was right about that. The algorithm — and the evidence — never lied. He did."
Now reveal the player accusations. Read each one aloud. Celebrate correct answers. For partially correct answers (right person, wrong weapon), give partial credit.
5
The Epilogue — What Happened to Everyone
~5 MIN
Only read epilogue entries for suspects whose secrets players actually uncovered during play. This rewards thorough investigation.
▶
Victor Delgado — Arrested on July 24, 2025. Charged with first-degree murder, extortion, and identity fraud. The wire fraud conviction from 2011 was re-entered into the record. Faces 30+ years. Forever Match was cancelled the same day.
▶
Vivian Fong — Cooperated fully with prosecutors. Pleaded guilty to wire fraud and misrepresentation. Received a reduced sentence and industry ban. She gave investigators the USB drive that broke the case.
▶
Solara Entertainment Group — Filed for bankruptcy protection following the EROS fraud disclosure. Three executives indicted. The $4.2M "AI infrastructure" was sold at auction for $31,000.
▶
Kavya, Dominic, Rafael, Zara — Each cleared completely. Their private secrets remained private. A settlement was reached with all contestants. Dominic and Zara were later seen together at a charity event in New York.
▶
Solomon Vance — His full name, Malik Bernard Vance, was published posthumously. The SEC investigation was quietly closed. His family established a technology ethics fellowship in his name at Howard University.
📢 GM Closing Line
"The Algorithm Never Lies. That was the show's tagline. And in the end — it was true. Every piece of digital evidence, every logged keycard entry, every recovered message — they all told the same story. The only one who lied was the man who built his entire life on a lie. Victor Delgado thought he had created the perfect crime. He forgot one thing: algorithms leave traces. And so do killers."
